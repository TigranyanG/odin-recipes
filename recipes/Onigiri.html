Onigiri

Description

Onigiri are Japanese rice balls, often shaped into triangles or rounds and wrapped in nori (seaweed). They're a staple snack and lunchbox item in Japan, beloved for their portability and simplicity.

They can be filled with ingredients like tuna mayo, pickled plum, or salmon, or left plain with just a sprinkle of salt. Their charm is in the balance of soft rice, savory filling, and crisp nori.

Ingredients

  • Cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • Salt
  • Nort sheets
  • Fillings

Steps

  1. Cook rice and let cool slightly.
  2. Wet hands, sprinkle with salt.
  3. Chill for 30 min.
  4. Serve with warm pita.
